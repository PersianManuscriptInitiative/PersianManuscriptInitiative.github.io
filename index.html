Persian Manuscript Initiative (PMI)

Preserving Persian culture, building the infrastructure for digital Persian manuscript studies

About Us

The Persian Manuscript Initiative (PMI) is a venture of the Roshan Institute for Persian Studies at the University of Maryland (College Park) dedidcated to the preservation and digital enhancement of Persian manuscripts throughout the world.

Through a unique collaboration with the Hill Museum and Manuscript Library—global leaders in the digital preservation of endangered manuscripts—we are currently working on Persian manuscript projects in India and Afghanistan. We are always open to new collaborations, so please get in touch if you have an idea for a new project.

Our Areas of Focus

Digitization: Working with global leaders in manuscript digitization, PMI will expand the number and quality of digitized Persian manuscripts, focusing in particular on archives in which the manuscripts are in danger due to political instability or lack of funding for proper preservation.

Education and Training: PMI is developing online training modules for teaching both manuscript studies and best practices for preservation and cataloging of Persian manuscripts.

Constructing Digital Infrastructure and Cultivating a Global Community of Users: PMI will develop the digital infrastructure to increase global access to Persian manuscripts. The development of this infrastructure will be coupled with a long-term plan for cultivating a committed global community of scholars and curators that will together increase our knowledge of the Persian manuscript tradition.
